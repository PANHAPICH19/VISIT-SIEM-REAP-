TOURIST PLACE

Angkor Wat

approximately 5.5 kilometers (about 3.4 miles) north of the town

Angkor Wat is a massive temple complex in Cambodia, originally built in the early 12th century as a Hindu temple dedicated to the god Vishnu. It later transformed into a Buddhist temple. Covering over 162 hectares, it is the largest religious monument in the world and is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and vast moats. Angkor Wat is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of Cambodia's cultural heritage, attracting millions of visitors each year. Its design represents an earthly model of the universe and showcases the ingenuity of Khmer architecture.

Beng Mealea or Boeng Mealea

40 km east of the main group of temples at Angkor and 67 km from Siem Reap
Located 40 kilometers east of Siem Reap, is a 12th-century temple overgrown by jungle, offering a mysterious and adventurous exploration.
Thought to be a prototype for Angkor Wat, it provides a raw and intimate experience for those seeking history and natural beauty away from the crowds.
It provides a quiet, off-the-beaten-path experience with a blend of history and nature.

Banteay Srei Temple

Located 25 kilometers northeast of Siem Reap

Is a 10th-century Hindu temple famous for its exquisite pink sandstone carvings.
Dedicated to Shiva, it is renowned for its detailed and artistic bas-reliefs, providing a serene and unique experience within the Angkor complex.
Best time to visit early morning or late afternoon is the best time to visit Banteay Srei to avoid the crowds and see the temple in the best light.

Bakong Temple

Around 15 km east of the Siem Reap city center
One of the oldest temples in the Angkor region, Bakong is the main temple of the Roluos Group, which predates Angkor Wat.
It is a pyramid-shaped temple with five tiers, and it's often considered the prototype for later Khmer temples.
The best time to visit Bakong Temple is early in the morning or late in the afternoon .These times allows you to avoid the midday heat and crowds

Pre Rup Temple

8 kilometers southeast of Siem Reap in the Angkor Archaeological Park
A pyramid-shaped temple in Angkor Archaeological Park.
Constructed from red sandstone, it features five towers in a square layout symbolizing Mount Meru.
The temple, known for its detailed Hindu carvings, was used for royal ceremonies and funerary rites. It's well-preserved and offers panoramic views, making it a popular sunset spot.

Kampong Phluk

Located in the Prasat Bakong District of Siem Reap Province
Kampong Phluk is a fascinating floating village located on the Tonle Sap Lake in Siem Reap, Cambodia. It's a unique community where people live on stilts that rise above the water level especially during the rainy season , where visitors can explore stilt houses and experience local ife on the water. The name "Kampong Phluk" means "Harbor of the Tusks.

Angkor Wildlife & Aquarium

National Road 6A, Khnar Pou Village, Khnar Pou Commune, Siem Reap
Open Hours : 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM Daily
A new attraction showcasing Cambodia's wildlife and marine life.
It features diverse freshwater and saltwater species, interactive touch tanks, and feeding sessions. The facility emphasizes conservation and offers educational programs, making it a family-friendly destination.

Phnom Krom Viewpoint

10km south of Siem Reap town along the Road 63
Best Time to Visit: Late Afternoon for Sunset
It is 30 minute drive from Siem Reap and then a short and steep walk up to the top. The temples itself are not very impressive after visiting Angkor Wat. But if offers stunning views over the nearby villages and the surrounding wetlands of Tonle Sap. You should come here to see the sunset! As the entry fee is already included within the Angkor Pass, it is a good half-day option to calm down after exhausting days in Angkor.

APOPO - Humanitarian Demining

Koumai Road Siem Reap, 17253
Open Hours : 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM Daily
APOPO is a non-profit that trains African giant pouched rats, called HeroRATs, to detect landmines and tuberculosis. Founded in 1997 by Bart Weetjens, APOPO operates in countries like Tanzania, Mozambique, and Cambodia. The rats are lightweight, making them ideal for detecting explosives without triggering them.

ភូមិព្រះដាក់ Phum Preah Dak

National Road 6A, Khnar Pou Village, Khnar Pou Commune, Siem Reap
Phum Preah Dak is a traditional village near the Angkor Archaeological Park in Siem Reap, renowned for its rich cultural heritage and artisanal craftsmanship. The village is famous for producing nom banh chok (Khmer noodles), and visitors can observe the process of making these noodles by hand. With its serene rural atmosphere, surrounded by rice fields and fruit trees, Phum Preah Dak offers an authentic glimpse into Cambodian village life. Its proximity to ancient temples also makes it a perfect spot for blending cultural exploration with historical sightseeing.

សច្ចៈ - Satcha Café & Restaurant

Street BBU Rd, Satcha Handicraft Center, Krong Siem Reap 17252
Open Hours: Mon-Sun 8:00 AM - 7:00 PM
Satcha Café & Restaurant is located in Siem Reap, Cambodia, and offers a unique blend of café and restaurant services along with an immersive cultural experience. It is part of the Satcha Handicraft Center, a venue dedicated to preserving and promoting traditional Cambodian crafts. The café serves as a tranquil spot where visitors can relax with local cuisine while also exploring the rich artisanal heritage of the region.
